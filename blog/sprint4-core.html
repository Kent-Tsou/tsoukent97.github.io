Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset



Principles of Neuroplasticity


Neuroplasticity can be split into two parts, neuro and plasticity. Neural means that it's related to the nervous system, while plasticity means that there is room for change and adaptivity. Human brains can encode experiences and learn new behaviours. On the other hand, it is also a mechanism when the damaged brain learns lost behavior in response to rehabilitation. The brain forms new neurons and lay down connections with the neurons, in response to learning and repeated behaviours. Neuroplasticity is very experience-dependant, in which some prioritise over others.


Factors that influence the facilitation of neuroplasticity include the following:


  1. 1. Use it or lose it - failure to practice what you learn can lead to loss of abilities.
  2. 2. Use it and improve it - applying training to what you have learnt can improve abilities.
  3. 3. Specifiy - the nature of training experience changes the development of the brain
  4. 4. Repetition matters - change can happen significantly if done sufficient enough of times.
  5. 5. Intensity matters - change requires intense training.
  6. 6. Time matters - different forms of change happen at different times during training.
  7. 7. Salience matters - the training must be meaningful in order to form change.
  8. 8. Age matters - training-induced change occurs more readily in younger brains.
  9. 9. Transference - Change in function as a result of one training experience can even lead to learning other similar skills.
  10. 10. Interference - Brain changes that result in bad habits can interfere with learning good habits.

Benefits of Neuroplasticity


The more the brain is exercised, the stronger and more connected it becomes. The more you practice what you love to do, the better you're going to become at it. Physical activity is one of the factors associated with positive neuroplasticity and is the primary focus of the training relationship. Study says that with conistent physical leisure neuroplasticity is optimized and people accelerate in their fields faster with consistent exercise.

Growth Mindset


Studies have shown that people who ha vethe belief that they are in control of their own ability, and can learn and improve those abilities. As cheesy as it sounds, having underlying belief that you are in control of your own destiny is the key to success.


A few ways to develop a growth mindset include:


  1. 1. Acknowledge and embrace imperfections.
  2. 2. View challenges as opportunities.
  3. 3. Try different learning tactics.
  4. 4. Replace the word “failing” with the word “learning.”
  5. 5. Stop seeking approval.
  6. 6. Value the process over the end result.
